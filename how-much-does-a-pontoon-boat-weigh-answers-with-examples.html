How much does a Pontoon Boat weigh? Answers with Examples

August 17, 2024

There is no specific weight as regards this pontoon boats  because they are different types. So each type of pontoon boat will most likely have a different weight in comparison with another one. 

This is one of the most frequently asked questions as regards pontoon boats, especially for those who are already familiar with different types of boats,ships and so on. 

However, for a novice, the most important question on his or her mind is; what is a pontoon boat? 

A pontoon boat is a very flat  boat that depends on floats to remain buoyant. These pontoons (also called tubes) contain much reserve buoyancy and allow designers to create massive deck plans fitted with all sorts of accommodations such as expansive lounge areas, stand-up bars, and sun pads. 

Better tube designs have allowed builders to put ever-increasing amounts of horsepower on the stern. The drafts may be as shallow as eight inches, which reduces risk of running aground and underwater damage, this allows it to come close to shore to pick up and drop off loads.

It was first invented by a farmer called Ambrose Weeres who lived close to Richmond, Minnesota in the United States. The name of the first boat was called The Empress. 

He went on to build more boats due to high demand for his pontoon boats and he was even given the nickname “Mr. Pontoon.” He was elected into the Minnesota Marina Hall of Fame afterward.

How Much does a Pontoon Boat weigh? 

A good number of pontoon boats weigh around 2,200 pounds (998 kilos) including the motor, and hold around 2,000 pounds (907 kg) of people and gear. A pontoon boat trailer weighs almost 1,200 pounds (550 kilos).
