Elfrid Payton’s Basketball and College Careers

Elfrid Payton attended Jefferson Parish High School in Jefferson Parish, Louisiana. Following an impressive rookie season in 2011-12, he enrolled at Louisiana Lafayette. Elfrid Payton was a sophomore at University in 2012-13.

Per game, he averaged 15.9 points, 5.6 rebounds, 5.5 assists, and 2.4 steals. Elfrid Payton was named to the Sun Belt Conference’s First Team. Payton was the final name on Team USA’s U19 World Championships Trials Roster for the 2013 FIBA U19 World Championship.

Payton managed to earn a gold medal with a team, going 9-0 throughout the event. As a junior in 2013-14, Payton established himself as one of the best defensive players in college basketball. He increased his per-game averages to 19.2 points, 5.9 assists, 6.0 rebounds, and 2.3 steals.

Payton was voted the Lefty Driesell Award winner as the National Defensive Player of the Year at the end of the season. Payton declared for the NBA draft in April 2014, foregoing his last year of college eligibility.

A career in the National Team

Payton was a last-minute addition to the US team for the 2013 FIBA Under-19 World Championship.

In nine tournament games, he averaged 6.1 points and 3.3 rebounds.

Professional Carrer

In June 2014, the Philadelphia 76ers selected Elfrid Payton with the 10th overall choice in the 2014 NBA Draft. On draft night, he was dealt with the Orlando Magic in exchange for Dario Saric, a future first and second-round pick.

Payton agreed to terms with the Orlando Magic on a rookie scale contract. He had a season-high 22 points and 12 assists against the Memphis Grizzlies in January 2015.

Payton was selected Eastern Conference Rookie of the Month in January and a Rising Stars Challenge participant. On March 18, he had his first triple-double with 15 points, 12 assists, and 10 rebounds in a loss to the Dallas Mavericks.

On March 20, he again had another triple-double. He became the first Magic player to record a triple-double in a row.

He was the first NBA rookie to accomplish this feat since Antoine Walker in 1997. Payton set a career-best with 24 points in a 104-101 victory over the Minnesota Timberwolves in November 2015.

Furthermore,

In a 118-112 loss against the Detroit Pistons in March 2016, he recorded his third triple-double with 20 points, 10 assists, and 10 rebounds.

In January 2017, he set a career-high with 28 points, nine rebounds, and nine assists against the Utah Jazz.

Similarly, he continues to rack up triple-doubles. He recorded his fourth triple-double in the last ten games. He averaged 20 points, 11 assists, and 11 rebounds in April, including his fifth triple-double of the season against the Brooklyn Nets.

Elfrid Payton missed the first eight games of the 2017-18 season due to a left hamstring injury.

The Phoenix Suns

In February 2018, Elfrid Payton was dealt with the Phoenix Suns in exchange for a 2018 second-round selection. He made his Phoenix Suns debut, averaging 19 points, nine assists, and six rebounds against the Denver Nuggets.

Payton then went on to post a triple-double against the Utah Jazz, tallying 13 points, 11 rebounds, and 12 assists. He was one of the Phoenix Suns’ few players to post three triple-doubles in a row.

The Pelicans of New Orleans

Payton agreed to terms with the New Orleans Pelicans in July of 2018. In his debut game with the New Orleans Pelicans in October 2018, he averaged 10 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists in a 131-112 victory over the Houston Rockets.

With 10 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists, he recorded his first triple-double for the Pelicans. It was his 11th triple-double of the season. In November, he was ruled out for six weeks owing to a damaged right ankle and a fractured finger.

After missing 22 games, he returned in December against the Minnesota Timberwolves. In a game against the Milwaukee Bucks, he had his second consecutive triple-double with 14 points, a career-high 15 rebounds, and 11 points.

He became the first New Orleans Pelicans player since Chris Paul in 2008 to have a triple-double in consecutive games. Payton joined Magic Johnson, Michael Jordan, James Harden, and Russell Westbrook as the only players since 1983-84 to record a triple-double in four consecutive games.

Payton scored 19 points, 11 assists, and 10 rebounds in a 129-125 overtime win over the Dallas Mavericks two days later, his fifth consecutive triple-double. He also equaled Chris Paul for the most scorers in a season in franchise history with his sixth triple-double.

Knicks of New York

Payton agreed to terms with the New York Knicks on a two-year contract in July 2019. At the start of the season, he was ruled out for 17 straight games.

Payton had his 17th triple-double of his career in February 2020, with 17 points, 11 rebounds, and 15 assists in a 139-134 overtime win over the Cleveland Cavaliers. In November 2020, he was waived by the New York Knicks. Payton was re-signed by the New York Knicks on a rumored one-year contract.

Returning to the Phoenix Suns

Elfrid Payton agreed to terms with the Phoenix Suns in August of 2021. From 2021 to the present, he has been a member of the Phoenix Suns.
