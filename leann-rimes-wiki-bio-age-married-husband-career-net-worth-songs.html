LeAnn Rimes’ Married Life and Husband

At Present, LeAnn Rimes and Eddie Cibrian are in a married relationship. Before Rimes met Cibrian, she was already a married woman.

Her first marriage was to Dean Sheremet. He was a backup dancer whom she met while hosting the 2001 Academy of Country Music Awards. The two fell in love and walked down the aisle the following year.

Rimes told InStyle Magazine that she wanted to marry him after her first date and they married in 2002. But they parted ways in July 2009 and announced their divorce in September 2009.

The divorce was finalized on June 19, 2010, exactly six months after Sheremet filed the divorce documents for dissolution of marriage.

The marriage had ended because of her extramarital affair with Eddie Cibrian while she worked with him on Northern Lights. Brandi Glanville, Cibrian’s ex-wife also filed for divorce as a result of the adultery in August 2009. This ended their eight years of marriage.

In June 2010, Rimes talked about the end of her first marriage stating;

“I take responsibility for everything I’ve done. I hate that people got hurt, but I don’t regret the outcome.”

On December 27, 2010, it was announced via Billboard that Rimes and Cibrian engaged. Rimes and Cibrian wed on April 22, 2011, at a private home in California, in front of a few friends and family members.

After that, her profession has frequently been eclipsed by the open disputes between them and Cibrian’s ex-wife, Brandi Glanville.

LeAnn Rimes Wiki-Bio; Age, Early Life, and Education

LeAnn Rimes born Margaret LeAnn Rimes was born on August 28, 1982, in Jackson, Mississippi. Wilbur Rimes is her father and Belinda Rimes is her mother.

They raised her in Dallas, Texas, where she began singing and dancing in local variety shows at the age of 5. Her first major musical theatre performance was in a Christmas carol put up by a Dallas Texas production.

She also participated in a Television competition show Star Search created by Alfred Masini and hosted by Ed McMahon. After it, Rimes decided to stick with country music. She toured around the nation performing in several states by the time she was nine years old.

LeAnn Rimes appeared on Johnnie High’s Country Music Revue, which gained popularity and gave Rimes the platform and publicity she needed at the time. Through her performances, she quickly built a following in Texas and garnered the attention of Dallas record promoter and DJ Bill Mack.

In 1994, the 11-year-old singer recorded her first album, All That, with the independent label Nor Va Jak. Later throughout her career, LeAnn Rimes collaborated with artists like Danielle Bregoli and also has a remastered duet with Elvis Presley.
